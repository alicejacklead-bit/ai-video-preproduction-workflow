# Camera Blocking

## Spatial Continuity Rule

The entire outro happens on one forward axis:

- the heroine starts far inside the tunnel
- she rides straight toward the lens
- the digital frame barrier exists just in front of the camera plane
- after impact, the bike exits camera-right foreground
- the pixel debris is then pulled back to center for logo formation

This keeps the action simple, iconic, and highly repeatable.

## Camera Setups

### CAM 1 | Tunnel Approach Master

- position: low frontal angle on tunnel centerline
- height: near rail / floor level
- lens feel: wide but not distorted
- shot size: long shot to medium-fast push-in feel created by subject approach
- subject: heroine bike approaching from far distance
- movement: mostly locked or subtly retreating, allowing speed to come from the subject
- purpose: establish the rider, the tunnel, and the attack line directly at camera

### CAM 2 | Hero Read Insert

- position: same axis, slightly higher and tighter as the bike closes in
- height: headlight to eye line
- lens feel: medium telephoto compression for impact
- shot size: tight front three-quarter insert
- subject: purple eyes, silver ponytail, front fairing glow
- movement: brief fast stabilization with cinematic vibration
- purpose: confirm identity before the collision

### CAM 3 | Frame-Break Impact

- position: camera at the barrier plane
- height: center-mass alignment with the motorcycle front
- lens feel: dynamic near-contact lens
- shot size: medium to extreme near-frame impact
- subject: front of the motorcycle hitting the transparent pixel border
- movement: tiny jolt on impact, then whip as bike exits right
- purpose: make the signature action unforgettable and readable

### CAM 4 | Debris Drift Suspension

- position: same camera-world orientation after the bike passes
- height: logo center level
- lens feel: medium
- shot size: centered graphic space
- subject: suspended blue-violet pixel shards and digital particles
- movement: minimal float, then inward pull
- purpose: create the transition from explosive action to controlled brand assembly

### CAM 5 | Logo Reassembly Lockoff

- position: centered straight-on graphic frame
- height: logo center
- lens feel: neutral medium
- shot size: full logo composition with comfortable margins in 4:3
- subject: text rebuild and purple elf-eye emblem
- movement: almost locked, only internal motion from particles and scan-light
- purpose: maximize readability and brand stability

## Beat-to-Camera Mapping

- 0.0-1.4s: CAM 1
- 1.4-2.0s: CAM 2
- 2.0-3.0s: CAM 3
- 3.0-3.4s: CAM 4
- 3.4-5.0s: CAM 5

## Blocking Warnings

- do not cut to side chase coverage before the impact; the frontal approach is the identity
- do not place the barrier deep in the tunnel; it must read as a screen-plane break
- do not let the bike cover the logo area during the final rebuild
- do not let particles remain chaotic after the text becomes readable
- do not center the bike exit downward or leftward; exiting to the right creates a clean wake vector for reassembly
