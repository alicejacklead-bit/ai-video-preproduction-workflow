# Continuity Proof

## Goal

Validate the four most failure-prone continuity points before final generation.

## Proof Shot 1 | Character and Vehicle Identity

- test frame: late CAM 1 or early CAM 2
- what to verify:
  - silver-white high ponytail
  - purple eyes
  - elf ears visible
  - deep-blue future sport motorcycle with cyan accents
- must pass:
  - no helmet appears
  - no character swap or face drift
  - no motorcycle redesign

## Proof Shot 2 | Digital Barrier Readability

- test frame: mid CAM 3 just before impact
- what to verify:
  - the barrier is visible as a transparent pixel mesh or digital border
  - it reads as a screen-plane element, not a wall in the tunnel
- must pass:
  - no ordinary glass appearance
  - no solid wall collision
  - no HUD overlay replacing the barrier concept

## Proof Shot 3 | Signature Impact and Exit Direction

- test frame: late CAM 3
- what to verify:
  - the bike hits center frame
  - pixel shards burst outward
  - the motorcycle sweeps past to camera-right
  - cyan slipstream remains visible after exit
- must pass:
  - impact feels forceful but clean
  - debris belongs to the broken barrier
  - bike exit direction stays consistent with the later pullback motion

## Proof Shot 4 | Logo Reassembly Order

- test sequence: CAM 4 into CAM 5
- what to verify:
  - debris pauses briefly
  - debris is drawn back inward by wake motion
  - `15S` forms first
  - `FRAME BREAK` forms second and largest
  - `15秒破帧行动` appears third
  - `MADE BY ANYFAST` appears fourth
  - purple elf-eye emblem lights last
- must pass:
  - all final text is readable
  - no garbled letters
  - no heavy particles blocking the logo
  - final frame holds stable for at least 0.5 seconds

## Pass Criteria

- one tunnel only
- one heroine only
- one locked bike design only
- digital barrier is not mistaken for glass
- shattered particles clearly become the final logo
- final layout is clean enough for recurring branded reuse

## If Something Fails

1. reduce effect density before adding more spectacle
2. restate that the barrier is a transparent digital pixel border, not glass
3. simplify the logo build order instead of speeding it up
4. protect the final 0.5-second hold above all else
