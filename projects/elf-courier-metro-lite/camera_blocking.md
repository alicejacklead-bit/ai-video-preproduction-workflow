# Camera Blocking

## Spatial Continuity Rule

The full video stays on one chase axis inside one subway tunnel.

- forward direction: heroine moves toward the oncoming train
- rear direction: pursuer follows from behind
- train direction: directly toward the heroine from ahead
- escape path: narrow strip beside the train exterior and tunnel wall
- post-pass area: heroine clears the train and reaches the gate trigger zone

## Camera Setups

### CAM 1

- position: low behind heroine bike, slightly right of center
- height: near wheel level
- lens feel: wide and fast
- direction: forward on chase axis
- shot size: moving medium-wide
- subject: heroine bike with pursuer visible behind
- movement: stabilized ground-hugging pursuit
- purpose: establish speed, tunnel continuity, and distance threat

### CAM 2

- position: near heroine shoulder / mirror line
- height: upper bike body level
- lens feel: medium insert
- direction: backward glance through rear-view mirror
- shot size: insert close-up
- subject: mirror reflection of the closing pursuer
- movement: short vibration-heavy tracking insert
- purpose: confirm the pursuer is gaining ground

### CAM 3

- position: ahead of heroine on chase axis
- height: headlight level
- lens feel: wide frontal compression
- direction: facing back toward heroine and train approach line
- shot size: wide to medium
- subject: heroine committing forward as train enters frame behind camera line
- movement: rapid pullback
- purpose: reveal the oncoming train threat without flipping space

### CAM 4

- position: tight insert on heroine face and right hand
- height: eye level and handlebar level
- lens feel: close-up
- direction: profile-biased
- shot size: extreme close-up
- subject: glowing eye, thumb pressing control
- movement: minimal, impact-focused
- purpose: show decision and trigger setup

### CAM 5

- position: side geography angle parallel to train side
- height: bike mid-body level
- lens feel: medium-wide
- direction: lateral tracking along the train exterior
- shot size: wide action profile
- subject: heroine pixel-phasing through the narrow side gap
- movement: rapid lateral follow
- purpose: prove the pass path is physical and readable

### CAM 6

- position: just behind the failed gap entry point, angled toward pursuer
- height: low near rail level
- lens feel: dynamic medium
- direction: toward the pursuer braking zone
- shot size: medium action
- subject: pursuer emergency drift stop with sparks
- movement: short whip then hold
- purpose: show why he cannot follow

### CAM 7

- position: far side of the train beyond the pass
- height: upper torso level
- lens feel: medium close
- direction: back toward the blocked path
- shot size: medium rear three-quarter to close-up
- subject: heroine look-back, glowing eye, button press, gate drop
- movement: brief settle then impact cut
- purpose: deliver payoff and lock the final geography

## Shot Transition Logic

- CAM 1 establishes the chase axis
- CAM 2 confirms threat closing from behind
- CAM 3 reveals the train ahead
- CAM 4 confirms heroine intent
- CAM 5 proves the narrow side-gap pass
- CAM 6 shows the pursuer forced to stop
- CAM 7 resolves control and trap on the far side

## Blocking Warnings

- Do not reverse the chase axis after CAM 1
- Do not stage the train on a random side in later shots
- Do not show the heroine leaving the bike
- Do not cut so tightly during CAM 5 that the side gap becomes unreadable
